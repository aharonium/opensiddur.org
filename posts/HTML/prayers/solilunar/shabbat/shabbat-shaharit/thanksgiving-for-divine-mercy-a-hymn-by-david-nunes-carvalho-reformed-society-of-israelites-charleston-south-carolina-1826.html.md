"Thanksgiving for Divine Mercy" appears as Hymn 21 in <em><a href="/?p=39485">The Sabbath service and miscellaneous prayers, adopted by the Reformed society of Israelites, founded in Charleston, S.C., November 21, 1825</a></em> (1830, Bloch: 1916), p. 64. According to Isaac Harby the hymn is an adaptation of Psalms 91. (In this statement there must be some confusion as I have difficulty locating any connection between this hymn and the psalm. Rather, this appears to be an adaptation of the Modim prayer in the Amidah.)
