TWILIGHT
How beautiful the daylight melts away, Leaving no wish for one returning ray Of the departed light; as twilight now, Like a sweet saint, with modest, pearly brow, Advances slowly; casting her pure hood Of softened light around us; while the good, The tender, gentle thoughts, and most refined, Steal o'er the soul, like summer's evening wind.
It is a time of bliss, the most subdued, And therefore perfect, then the soul's imbued With holiness serene, with wisdom bright, Which shines not always in less tender light.
It is a time when all that's harsh must flee, Must yield to soft, bewitching harmony.
The poem, "Twilight" by Rosa Emma Salaman, was first published in the Occident 7:5, Ab 5609/August 1849, p.245.
